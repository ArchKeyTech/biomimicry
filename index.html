What is Biomimicry ?

Like/Unlike
Save text for later

Biomimicry is a creative and intelligent process that emulates strategies found in nature to offer sustainable solutions for design challenges faced by humans.

The key word is sustainability. Unlike other bio-inspired designs, biomimicry places a larger emphasis on solutions mimicking nature in terms of its functionality.

By closely studying and learning from our ecosystem we have been able to create regenerative designs that fully emulate distinct properties found throughout nature in a manner that's aligned with the ethos of biomimicry.

How is Biomimicry applied ?

Like/Unlike
Save text for later

Researchers and innovators carefully apply biomimicry principles to solve problems across various disciplines such as medicine, robotics, architecture, agriculture etc.

There are 3 fundamental ideas to biomimicry:

Emulation:

Using scientific methods combined with an understanding of the ecosystem to mimic nature's approach in problem solving.

Ethos:

Ensuring that the philosophy behind the aspects of all designs are supportive and contribute to enriching conditions of life and not deteriorate it.

(Re)Connect:

Rebuilding a bond with a nature to better understand it and align our design principles around it.

Biomimicry allows us to address challenges as efficiently and sustainably as nature does. Explore biomimicry further by checking out the Gallery.
